JPG's are designed to handle larger color pallets without a big size;

GIF's are a go-to when using simple animations, but the tradeoff is that they have pretty bad color pallete and details;

PNG's are great for anything that's not a photo or animated. They don't have color limitations, and they do deal with opacity.

Unlike all previous formats, SVG is a vector-based graphics format, which means they can be scaled to any size without loss of quality, try to use them always.
